üìñ What is a Cricket Score?

A cricket score shows the performance of two teams playing against each other. Each score displays the number of runs scored and the number of wickets lost.

Team A: 250/5 vs Team B: 180/8

This means Team A scored 250 runs with 5 wickets lost, while Team B scored 180 runs with 8 wickets lost.

üî¢ Understanding Score Notation

Symbol Meaning Example
382/7 Runs scored / Wickets lost 382 runs, 7 wickets lost
255/10 Complete innings (all wickets lost) 255 runs, all 10 wickets lost (match ended)
v Versus (against) India v Pakistan
& And (separator between innings) 350/7 & 245/5 (two different innings)
* Currently batting (LIVE) India 150/3 * (India is batting right now)

üìä Match Status Indicators

üü¢ Live Match LIVE

An asterisk (*) indicates the match is currently ongoing. One team is actively batting right now.

‚ö´ Completed Match COMPLETED

No asterisk means the match is finished or paused. Both teams have completed their turns or the match has ended.

‚ùå All Out (10/10)

When a team loses all 10 wickets, it shows /10. Their batting turn is over.

‚ö†Ô∏è In Progress

If the score shows less than /10 with an asterisk, the team is still batting and can score more runs.

‚ö° Quick Tips for Reading Scores

‚úì Tip 1: The higher the runs, the better the performance (generally).

‚úì Tip 2: Fewer wickets lost means the team is playing well. Losing 10 wickets means the innings is over.

‚úì Tip 3: If you see a * (asterisk), that team is CURRENTLY BATTING - the match is LIVE!

‚úì Tip 4: The & symbol shows different innings (rounds) of the same team in longer format matches.

‚úì Tip 5: Compare runs and wickets: More runs + fewer wickets lost = Team is winning!

üéØ Common Match Formats

1. Test Matches: Teams get multiple innings with & symbol (e.g., 350/7 & 245/6)

2. One Day International (ODI): Single innings per team (e.g., 280/8)

3. T20/T20I: Shortest format with fast-paced scores (e.g., 165/7)
